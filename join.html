FAQ

Is this another course I won't finish?
No, and that's a structural claim, not a pep talk. The OS works the day you paste it in; the training is depth, not a prerequisite. You could skip every video and still have a running Content OS. The 2x-weekly calls and the 30-day guarantee exist precisely for the version of you that's bought courses before.
Do I need to be technical? "Claude Code" sounds like coding.
If you can install an app, drop a file in a folder, and paste a command, you have the prerequisites. Nobody in here is writing code — you're directing agents in plain English. The install course walks it step by step, and same-day support catches you when something on your machine misbehaves. That's most of what support is.
Why not just hire an editor or an agency?
You can — it's $1,500–5,000/mo for someone else's judgment of your brand, and you're back to renting. This is a different bet: you own the operation, your voice is the constraint, and your monthly cost is a fraction of one freelancer. The honest trade-off: it takes your direction. If you truly want it handled end to end, that's the done-for-you build, not this.
I use Codex, not Claude Code. Am I out?
No. The Content OS is built for Claude Code first — that's where everything ships and gets tested — but it runs on Codex too, and the training covers both runtimes. Same files, same agents, same pipeline.
Can I run this for clients, or across multiple brands?
Yes — the OS is scope-locked per brand by design, so each client or brand gets its own clean installation with its own voice as the constraint. Several of the pipelines inside were built running client work. If you want us to build a client's system for them instead, that's the done-for-you build.

The smoke test at the end of the install course produces real content, and most members get there in their first sitting or two. Week one: scoped to your brand, first real batch. Day 30: a full month of content, and that's the line the guarantee is drawn on. Printed, not a vibe.
