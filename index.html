FAQ

Questions, answered.

What is Nave?

Nave is a plug-and-play Claude Code template. You clone the repo and run one command, and a whole content department installs onto your machine — a video editor, a publisher, comment/DM automation, a copywriter, a content coach, footage intelligence, a competitor scout, and a library of format-specialist agents. Drop a month of footage and it produces a month of finished, on-brand content. You own the system.

Do I need to know how to code?

No. A one-command installer sets up everything, and a five-minute bootstrap configures Nave to your brand. After that you talk to it in plain English — “make a month of content from this video.” There's also a done-with-you option where it's installed with you live.

How is it different from Blotato or other AI content tools?

Hosted tools are rented SaaS — you pay per credit and own nothing the day you stop paying. Nave is a template you own and run on your own Claude Code, with no per-credit markup. And instead of one generic repurpose, Nave is a format factory: one footage drop runs through a whole library of brand-tuned format-specialist agents, producing a slate of distinct, finished pieces.

What does “a month of content in 30 minutes” mean?

After setup, you give Nave one footage drop or video and it produces a month's slate — roughly 8–15 short clips, a few carousels, a long-form package, and a full posting schedule — staged for your approval. Your hands-on time is about 30 minutes of review; the agents do the rest.

What do I need to run it?

Claude Code and your own API keys (Anthropic for the agents, AssemblyAI for transcription, and a publishing key). The installer handles ffmpeg, the render engine, and the face-tracking reframe engine. You pay your own usage directly.

Who is Nave for?

People building with AI and Claude Code who want a proven content system they own — operators, freelancers, agency owners, and creators who'd rather run their own engine than rent another tool.
