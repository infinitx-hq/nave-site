Own, don't rent

Rent a repurposing tool, or own a content department.

Renting

Repurposing tools are great if renting repurposing is what you want. They're fast to start, they're polished, and there's a support team when you get stuck. You pay monthly, you work inside their templates, and the workflow lives on their servers. That's a fair trade if it's the trade you want.

🛸 Owning

With Nave you own the machine: the orchestration, the config, the agents, the local pipeline. You bring your own metered keys, so you pay cost instead of markup. You can swap any single tool without re-buying the system. The repo sits on your disk. Nobody can re-price it, throttle it, or lock you out of your own work.

What you still depend on: your Claude subscription, and the tool vendors you pay directly. Claude is metered too — usage caps on a shared pool, and full month-runs want Max.

FAQ

Every question worth asking before $397

Do I need an Anthropic API key?

No. Nave runs on your own Claude Code — your existing Claude subscription, no separate Anthropic API key, ever. You bring your own keys only for the tools it orchestrates: AssemblyAI (transcription) and Zernio (publishing + DM automation), plus optional OpenAI and xAI keys if you want the Competitor Scout's deep research. You pay that usage directly to those vendors. No markup, no credits bought through us.

Which Claude plan do I need?

Claude Max ($100/mo and up) is the recommended plan for full month-runs. A multi-agent monthly run can exhaust a $20 Pro plan's 5-hour usage window in under an hour, and that pool is shared with everything else you use Claude for. On Pro, run formats one at a time and expect the month to take a few sittings. Your Claude subscription is itself metered (usage caps, shared pool), so plan around that.

What does "a month of content in an hour" really mean?

It means one-time install first (that's the cohort), then about an hour of your hands-on time per month after that. The crew does the production. Your hour is the review: you check the staged queue and say "schedule it." The approval gate is part of the design.

Is the cohort 1:1?

No, it's a live group cohort, deliberately. We install together because the group is part of the value: same errors, fixed in front of everyone. The 1:1 layer is the Loom support — send your problem and your screen, and you get a custom walkthrough within 2 business days, as many rounds as it takes inside your guarantee window.

What exactly are the guarantees and refunds?

The $397 cohort carries the Get-Running Guarantee: Nave runs on your machine within 30 days of your cohort date, or we make it run, or you get your money back. Written terms here. The $89 template carries bounded install support instead: async Loom replies, up to 3 within 30 days of purchase, to get your installer verified and your first render out. No cash refund on the template: it's a forkable repo, and once cloned it can't be returned. Refund policy here.

What do I own, and what can still change on me?

You own the machine: orchestration, config, agents, the local pipeline, the repo on your disk. You bring your own metered keys, so you pay cost rather than markup, and you can swap any single tool without re-buying the system. What can still change on you: your Claude subscription (metered, usage caps, shared pool — Max for full runs) and the tool vendors you pay directly. Ownership here means no middleman and no re-pricing layer between you and your tools. It does not mean "no caps exist anywhere."

What machine do I need?

Windows, macOS, or Linux. Under the hood: Node 20+, Python 3.12, ffmpeg, yt-dlp, jq — and the installer checks and installs them for you (winget on Windows, Homebrew on macOS, apt on Debian-based Linux). It's safe to re-run and has a check-only mode, so you can verify your machine before the cohort. macOS needs Homebrew already installed; non-Debian Linux distros aren't auto-handled yet.

What happens after the founding window?

The community continues, and founding members keep the $89/mo rate for life. The rate goes up for everyone who joins after the founding window. The $397 founding price for the cohort install goes up too: the founding window closes when cohort 1 ships, and $397 is the lowest the cohort will ever be sold for.
