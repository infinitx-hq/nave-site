007 — FAQ

Real questions. Straight answers.

01Do I need an Anthropic API key?

No. Nave runs on your own Claude Code — your existing Claude subscription, no separate Anthropic API key, ever. You bring keys only for the tools it orchestrates: AssemblyAI (transcription) and Zernio (publishing + DM automation), plus optional OpenAI and xAI for deep research. You pay those vendors directly. No markup.

02Which Claude plan do I need?

Claude Max ($100/mo+) is recommended for full month-runs. A multi-agent run can exhaust a $20 Pro plan's 5-hour window in under an hour, and that pool is shared with everything else you use Claude for. On Pro: run formats one at a time, expect a few sittings.

03What does "a month of content in an hour" really mean?

One-time install first (that's the live session), then about an hour of your hands-on time per month. The crew produces; your hour is the review. You check the staged queue and say "schedule it." Nothing publishes without your approval.

04Is the install really 1:1?

Yes — one hour, live, just you and me, on your machine. We install, place your keys, verify a real render, set up your brand, and kick off your first month's run before we hang up. After the session: the weekly community call plus Loom support — your problem, your screen, a custom walkthrough within 2 business days.

05What are the guarantees and refunds?

The $397 install: Nave runs on your machine within 30 days of your session, or we make it run, or your money back — written terms. The $89 template: bounded install support (up to 3 Loom replies in 30 days), no cash refund — it's a forkable repo; once cloned it can't be returned. Refund policy.

06What do I own — and what can still change on me?

You own the machine: orchestration, config, agents, the local pipeline, the repo on your disk. You pay cost instead of markup and can swap any single tool without re-buying the system. What can still change: your Claude subscription (metered, usage caps) and the tool vendors you pay directly. Ownership means no middleman — not "no caps exist anywhere."

07What machine do I need?

Windows, macOS, or Linux. Under the hood: Node 20+, Python 3.12, ffmpeg, yt-dlp, jq — the installer checks and installs them for you, is safe to re-run, and has a check-only mode. macOS needs Homebrew; non-Debian Linux isn't auto-handled yet.

08Who should NOT buy Nave?

You, if: you don't film and won't start (Nave needs your footage — you drive, the system supports you) · you want 100% hands-off (nothing posts without your review) · you're on Claude Pro and won't upgrade (works, just slower) · you want it built and tuned for your business without touching it — that's the custom build, $2,500–$7,500 one-time.

09What happens after the founding window?

The $397 founding price goes to $497 when the founding window closes — $397 is the lowest the install will ever be sold for. Founding members keep their community access; whatever later members pay for it, it will be more than you did.
